Introduction

Hi, I'm Rongxin, and welcome!
I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Feinberg School of Medicine, Northwestern University. Prior to this, I received my Ph.D. from the State Key Laboratory of Digital Medical Engineering at Southeast University, during which I did part of my Ph.D. training in Dr. Jean-Louis Mergny's lab at École Polytechnique in France.
My Ph.D. projects involved uncovering the regulatory role of G-quadruplexes (G4s), special non-B nucleic acid secondary structures, and their implication in diseases.

Moving forward, and with the goal of unlocking the secrets hidden within the "Book of Heaven" (the human genome), my research interests lie in the analysis and interpretation of large-scale human multi-omics data through bioinformatics approaches. More specifically, I aim to understand and elucidate the regulatory mechanisms of the human genome‚Äîfor example, the impact of sequence variants and chromatin-level regulatory features on genome function‚Äîand how their dysregulation contributes to the development and progression of human diseases, such as cancer. In addition, I am passionate about applying machine learning methods to model and address unresolved challenges in biology and medicine.
